# The Developer Journey

A practical guide for aspiring developers — what to learn, what to avoid, projects to build, interview preparation, deployment knowledge, and lessons from my own journey into software engineering.

## Before You start

I’m not a writer, and this probably won’t sound extremely technical or perfectly polished. This is simply the story of how I navigated the software development jorney, the problems I ran into, the architecture decisions I made, and the lessons I learned while trying to make everything work in life.

## Why I Created This

If you’re reading this, you might wonder why I’m writing all of this in the first place.

The answer is simple: this is everything I wish someone had told me when I started my journey.

Software development can feel overwhelming in the beginning. There are thousands of tutorials, endless technologies, and everyone online somehow seems ten steps ahead of you. Most people only share the polished version — the successful deployment, the clean architecture diagram, the final product.

But very few talk about the confusion, the bad decisions, the deployment failures, the debugging at 3 AM, or the moments where you genuinely question whether you understand anything at all.

This blog is my attempt to document those parts too.

In some ways, it feels like writing a guide for my younger self — the version of me who was just getting started, trying to understand how real systems are built.

And honestly, if I ever got a time machine, I’d probably still choose software development again.

Because despite all the frustration, it’s beautiful.

The ability to build things from nothing, solve problems, connect systems together, and slowly watch an idea turn into something real is still one of the most rewarding things I’ve experienced.

So this is for anyone currently at the beginning of their journey.

- The people learning

- The people struggling

- The people building projects nobody sees yet

I hope something in this helps you move a little faster than I did.
